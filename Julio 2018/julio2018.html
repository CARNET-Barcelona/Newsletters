MaaS Catalonia
Launch of the MaaS Manifesto

MaaS Catalonia is an initiative launched with the main aim to generate a space for debate, expert analysis, knowledge sharing, development of opportunities and projects around MaaS from Catalonia, summoning all stakeholders – public and private – to participate and come up with proposals, in a constructive and open way, in a framework of cooperation.

Our first initiative has been launching the "MaaS Manifesto", on May 28, a document laying out 7 principles we, as MaaS Catalonia, consider as pillars to build MaaS from a high level perspective and, of course, subject to the constructive debate we wish to promote. With the "MaaS Manifesto", MaaS Catalonia initiates its activity, from the very beginning, seeking international cooperation with the widest spectrum of stakeholders.

ELISAVA JOINS CARNET

We welcome ELISAVA as a new CARNET industrial partner. Established in 1961, it is the first design school launched in Spain. ELISAVA is a leading institution in design studies, both at a local and international level, with a professional approach. A centre of excellence, a pioneer in providing the official degrees established by the European Higher Education Area (EHEA). ELISAVA promotes education, knowledge, research, development and innovation in the field of design, engineering and communication. The School offers a college education that prepares students to meet professional challenges worldwide.

ELISAVA is a School affiliated with Pompeu Fabra University, and offers official Bachelor’s Degrees in Design and Engineering in Industrial Design. It also offers professionally-oriented MA and Postgraduate Diploma programmes, and the Master’s Degree in Design and Communication, the first official Master’s Degree in Design in Spain. The School's offer is further supplemented with a wide range of specialised design courses for professionals and firms. All these learning paths are the result of new emerging careers, benefiting from the quality and creative personality that have given the School such prestige over the years.

ELISAVA's new headquarters are located in La Rambla in Barcelona, one of the most emblematic areas in the city, recognised internationally for its historic and cultural value and for its vitality. At the School there are over 2,000 students, and 700 lecturers and collaborators with extensive teaching experience and a solid professional background.

Over the years it has been collaborating with CARNET projects, being the most recent one a car-sharing design and business solutions, led by SEAT and Rucker Lypsa, where students presented their proposals as their Final Graduation Thesis.

KIC on Urban Mobility – CARNET is there!
The European Institute of Technlogy (EIT) has closed its call on a new KIC on Urban Mobility July 12th. CARNET was present during the VW Future Mobility Days

Each year the Volkswagen Group promotes a week of innovation where all Volkswagen brands show their most recent and disruptive projects. One of the projects that CARNET shown was the Autonomous Delivery Device which conceptualizes the future of the delivery services on crowded urban environments, investigating the requirements of potential operators and potential customers.
Second edition of the Autonomous Driving Challenge

CARNET hosted on May the Workshop from the Autonomous Driving Challenge, the second edition of a competition between Spanish Universities on autonomous cars. It has been two days filled with lectures and trials where 35 students received their 1:10 model cars and tested them in a collaborative environment.

Creative Lab 4 – Solving Autonomous Driving Interfaces & Multimodality

CARNET collaborated in June on the fourth edition of the Creative Lab, a permanent teaching and creativity method between UPC, Volkswagen Group Research and SEAT. Four groups of students from different bachelors from the UPC ETSEIAAT proposed solutions on augmented reality, multimodal advisor and sport driving for autonomous cars.
Final event for the inlane project
On 19 June, RACC will host the final event of INLANE, a European project focused on the integration of the new GALILEO satellite system and computer vision technology to develop a low-cost, lane-level, precise turn-by-turn navigation application. The Consortium is formed among others of TomTom, Honda, Vicomtech, Intel, Barcelona City Council and the RACC. The event will include a demonstration of this technology.

Altran adheres to the Andalusian Industry Pact

At the Fourth Edition of Aerospace and Defence Meetings 2018, held in Seville from 15–18 May, Altran adhered to the Andalusian Industry Pact, an agreement that is part of the Andalusian Industrial Strategy 2020 and has already been endorsed by 1,864 companies and institutions. The aim is to foster a strong, competitive, sustainable sector with companies that can create quality employment and international solvency.
